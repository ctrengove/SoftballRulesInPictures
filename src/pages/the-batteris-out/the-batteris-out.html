The Batter is Out

  1. A bunt is a batted ball not swung at but intentionally met with the bat and tapped slowly within the playing field.
  2. A fly ball is any ball batted into the air.
  3. The Batter is Out if he hits a Fair Fly Ball which is caught by a Fielder before it touches the ground.
  4. The Batter is out if he hits a Foul Fly Ball which is caught by a Fielder before it touches the ground.
  5. The Batter is out if his third Strike is caught by the catcher.
  6. If there is a Runner on first base with less than 2 out and the catcher drops the third Strike, the Batter is Out. This rule prevents an unfair double play.
  7. If the third Strike is dropped, with less than two out and first base is not occupied, the Batter-Runner is safe if he can beat the throw to first, or he is not tagged off base.
  8. The Batter is out if he bunts the third Strike Foul.
  9. The Batter is out if the third Strike is swung at and the ball hits him.
