Interference Plays

  1. On all Interference plays somebody must be out and the ball is dead. If the Batter-Runner interferes, Runners return to the last base touched at the time of the pitch. If a Runner interferes, Runners return to the last base touched at the time of the interference. Rule 9 Section 1f.
  2. The rule of thumb for deciding who is out on an interference is this:
  3. If the person who interfered can be called out (because they were a Batter, Batter-Runner or Runner who has not yet been put out – then they are out;
  4. If the person who interfered can’t be out (because they have already been called out or it was a coach, on-deck batter or other member of the offensive team) – then the runner closest to home is out.
  5. When the Batter interferes with a play at the plate, he is out. Runners return to the last base held at the time of the Interference.
  6. The Runner is out when he fails to yield right of way to a Fielder fielding a Fair Batted Ball.
  7. The Runner is out when he intentionally interferes with a thrown ball. In this case an interference had occurred and the ball is dead.
  8. The Runner closest to home is out if the coach leaves the coach’s box and makes the Fielder think he is a Runner by running toward home, and draws a throw from the Fielder to the catcher.
  9. The Runner is out if teammates gather around a base to which the Runner is advancing to confuse or hinder the Defensive Team.
