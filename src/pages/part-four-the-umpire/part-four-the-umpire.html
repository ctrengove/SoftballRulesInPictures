Part 4: The Umpire

  1. The umpire should line up with the eyes just above the top of the zone and on in the inside edge of the plate between the plate and the Batter.
  2. Umpires should judge Balls and Strikes over the catcher’s shoulder nearest to the Batter.
  3. Be in a position to call all plays. If working alone, always start behind the plate.
  4. Most amateur games use two umpires. One is always behind the plate. With nobody on base, the other umpire stands as shown, and moves around the diamond, no further than Shortstop, with the lead runner.
  5. "Balls" are not indicated, merely vocalised.
  6. Strikes are called verbally and signaled with the right arm.
  7. The umpire’s signal for an Out.
  8. The signal for Safe.
  9. So everyone knows the count, Indicate "balls" on the left hand and “Strikes” on the right hand. As a general rule, indicate the count when there are 2 strikes or 3 balls on the batter, or every 3 pitches to a batter, or when there’s been a break in play.
  10. Umpires are impartial. If you see an action that could be appealed, don't reveal what you saw until an appeal is properly made.
  11. Between innings, take a position approx. 8 feet from Home Plate down the Foul line on the side of the team in the field.
  12. When anyone questions a call, listen briefly, explain what you saw and explain the rule if you need to, then passively ask the player or coach to continue the game and start to move away.
  13. If the person continues to dispute the call, or verbally abuses you, then under the rules you may need to eject them from the game.
  14. When cleaning home plate, step inside the diamond, face the back net, turn your back to the pitcher, lean over and brush the plate. Try to keep the plate clean but don’t delay the game to do it.
