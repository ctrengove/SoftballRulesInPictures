Appeal Plays

  1. An Appeal must be made to the umpire by a Defensive Player before the next pitch to a Batter. An Appeal is defined by the rules as the act of a Fielder in claiming a violation of the rules by the Offensive Team.
  2. If a Runner leaves base before a Fly Ball, Fair or Foul, is first touched and caught, he is out if the base is tagged before he returns to the base.
  3. If a Runner leaves base before a Fly Ball, Fair or Foul, is first touched and caught, he is out if he is tagged before he returns to the base.
  4. If a Runner misses a base, he may be put out by a Fielder touching the base with the ball before the Runner retags the base, or he may be tagged out before he returns to the missed base.
  5. The Runner is out if he is tagged while returning to first base after over running and attempting to run to second base.
  6. The Runner is out if he over-slides home plate – misses it and makes no attempt to return to it – and the plate is tagged by a Fielder.
  7. All Runners must return to the last base held at the time of the pitch on a Foul ball not caught.
  8. The Runner need not retouch after a Foul tip as this is considered a regular Strike and the ball remains alive.
