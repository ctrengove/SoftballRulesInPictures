Batter Base Awards

  1. To be “awarded” a base means to be permitted to advance there without Jeopardy – that is, without danger of being put out.
  2. The Batter is awarded first base by having four balls called by the umpire.
  3. The Batter is awarded first base if the catcher Obstructs and prevents him hitting the ball safely. If he hits the ball safely and advances both himself and all other Runners, the obstruction is cancelled.
  4. The Batter is awarded first base when a Fair Batted Ball Strikes the umpire before touching or passing a Fielder, other than the pitcher.
  5. The Batter is awarded first base if struck by a pitched ball he is not attempting to hit and which he tries to avoid.
