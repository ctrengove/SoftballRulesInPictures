Part 2: The Batter

  1. A Batter is an Offensive Player who enters the Batter’s Box with the intention of aiding his team to obtain runs. He continues to be a Batter until he is either declared out by the umpire or he becomes a Batter-Runner.
  2. A Batter-Runner is a player who has finished a turn at bat but has not yet been put out, or touched first base.
  3. The Batter must stand with both feet within the Batter’s Box. Rule 7 Section 3c.
  4. Any ball at which the Batter swings and misses is a Strike, regardless of the position of the pitch.
  5. A "Ball" is called for each pitch that does not enter the Strike Zone and is not swung at.
  6. The Batter is out if a foot is completely outside the lines of the Batter’s Box and is touching the ground at the time he hits the ball.
  7. The Batter shall not change Batter’s Boxes while the pitcher is ready to pitch. Penalty: The Batter is out.
  8. Four Balls entitles the Batter to go to first base. This is called a Base on Balls.
  9. The Batter is permitted to go to first base if a pitched ball, that is not a Strike, hits him and he has not swung at it.
  10. It is a Strike if the ball is swung at and missed.
  11. It is a Strike and the ball is dead if the ball is swung at and missed, and the pitch hits the Batter. If the batter had 2 Strikes prior to being hit by the ball, they would also be out as it would be Strike 3.
  12. It is a Strike if the ball is hit into Foul Territory and is not caught and there are less than two Strikes on the Batter. If the Batter has 2 Strikes, the ball is dead and each runner must return to his base without liability to be put out. Rule 7 Section 4d and effect.
